# Freelance Services Agreement — Template

> **How to use this:** Copy everything below the line into a document. Fill in every
> `[BRACKET]`. Delete this box. Send it to the client *before* starting work; both
> of you sign (typed name + date is fine, or use a free e-sign tool like Documenso,
> Dropbox Sign, or even a signed photo). Keep a copy.
>
> ⚠️ **Not legal advice.** This is a plain-English starter that covers the basics
> for small freelance jobs. For large contracts, have a lawyer review it. Laws vary
> by country — the "Governing law" line should be your own location.

---

# SERVICES AGREEMENT

**Between:**
- **Service Provider:** Veer Sethi ("Provider") — [YOUR ADDRESS] — [YOUR EMAIL]
- **Client:** [CLIENT NAME / COMPANY] ("Client") — [CLIENT ADDRESS] — [CLIENT EMAIL]

**Date:** [DATE]

---

## 1. The work (Scope)
The Provider will deliver the following:

- [e.g. A 5-page website built on Next.js, mobile-responsive, with a contact form]
- [e.g. One automation that takes WhatsApp orders and logs them to Google Sheets]
- [list every deliverable clearly — this is what protects both sides]

**Not included:** anything not listed above is out of scope and quoted separately.

## 2. Timeline
- Start date: [DATE] (work begins once the deposit is received).
- Estimated delivery: [X days / by DATE].
- Delays caused by late feedback or late content from the Client extend the
  timeline accordingly.

## 3. Price & payment
- **Total project fee:** [$AMOUNT] (fixed).
- **Deposit:** [50%] = [$AMOUNT], due before work begins. **Non-refundable** once
  work has started.
- **Balance:** [50%] = [$AMOUNT], due on completion, before final files are
  handed over / the project goes live.
- Payment method: [bank transfer / Wise / PayPal / etc.].
- Invoices unpaid for more than [7] days may pause the project.

## 4. Revisions
- Includes [2] rounds of revisions within the agreed scope.
- Additional revisions or new requests are billed at [$RATE] or quoted separately.

## 5. Client responsibilities
The Client will provide, in good time: content, text, images, logins/access, and
timely feedback. The Provider isn't responsible for delays caused by missing items.

## 6. Ownership (IP)
- On **full and final payment**, all rights to the final delivered work transfer to
  the Client.
- Until full payment, all work remains the property of the Provider.
- The Provider may keep tools, code libraries, and general know-how used to build
  the work, and may **show the finished work in a portfolio** unless the Client
  asks otherwise in writing.

## 7. Third-party costs
Subscriptions or services needed to run the work (hosting, domains, API fees,
plugins, etc.) are **paid by the Client** and are not part of the project fee
unless stated in Scope.

## 8. Confidentiality
Both parties will keep each other's private business information confidential and
use it only for this project.

## 9. Warranty & support
- The Provider will fix bugs in the delivered work, free, for [14] days after
  delivery.
- After that, support is available under a separate maintenance arrangement.
- The work is provided "as is"; the Provider isn't liable for indirect losses, and
  total liability is limited to the fees actually paid under this agreement.

## 10. Cancellation
- Either party may cancel with written notice.
- If the Client cancels, the deposit is retained and any work completed beyond the
  deposit is invoiced.

## 11. Independent contractor
The Provider is an independent contractor, not an employee. The Provider handles
their own taxes.

## 12. Governing law
This agreement is governed by the laws of [YOUR COUNTRY / STATE].
