# Pricing & Negotiation Cheat-Sheet — Veer Sethi

A practical guide for quoting clients confidently. Keep it open during calls.

> Quick rule: **quote per project, not per hour.** Hourly punishes you for being
> fast and scares clients with open-ended bills. A fixed price you both agree on
> up front means certainty for them and a fair reward for your speed.

---

## 1. The 5-step pricing flow (every client)

1. **Listen first.** Let them describe the problem fully. Don't quote yet.
2. **Ask the money question early:** *"Do you have a rough budget in mind for this?"*
   Their answer tells you which tier to aim at.
3. **Scope it in your head** → match to a tier below.
4. **Send the price in writing** (after the call, not blurted on the call). A clean
   message or the contract. Always written.
5. **Hold your price.** If they push back, change the *scope*, not the number
   (see §4).

---

## 2. Starting prices by project type

These are *floors* for someone building a reputation — raise them as your
portfolio grows. (USD; adjust for the client's country — see §6.)

| Project | Typical range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1-page / landing site | $250 – $600 | Most common first job |
| Multi-page website (3–6 pages) | $700 – $2,000 | Your bread and butter |
| E-commerce store (Shopify/custom) | $1,200 – $4,000 | More if custom checkout/automation |
| Single automation (Zapier/Make/n8n) | $200 – $800 | Per workflow |
| Complex automation / custom script | $800 – $3,000 | Multiple steps, APIs, error handling |
| AI chatbot / agent | $500 – $2,500 | Depends on data + channels |
| Lead-gen / scraping pipeline | $400 – $1,500 | + optional monthly run fee |
| Dashboard / reporting | $600 – $2,500 | Data sources drive the price |
| **Monthly retainer** (support + small changes) | $150 – $800 / mo | Recurring income — pitch this! |

**Add-ons you can charge for:** rush delivery (+25–50%), extra revision rounds,
copywriting, logo/branding, ongoing hosting/maintenance, training session.

---

## 3. Payment terms (protect yourself)

- **50% upfront, 50% on delivery.** Non-negotiable for new clients. You don't
  start until the deposit lands.
- Larger projects (>$2,000): **3 payments** — 40% start / 30% midway / 30% on
  delivery.
- Retainers: **paid at the start of each month**, in advance.
- State clearly: **final files / go-live happen after final payment.**
- Late payment: it's fair to add *"invoices unpaid after 7 days pause the
  project."*

---

## 4. Negotiation — how to defend your price

People will ask for a discount. That's normal. Rules:

- **Never drop price without dropping scope.** *"I can do $X — or if budget's
  tight, we drop [feature] and it's $Y."* This protects your rate and gives them
  a real choice.
- **Anchor high, then offer the tier.** Mention the Custom option first so the
  Growth price feels reasonable.
- **Silence is a tool.** Say the price, then stop talking. Let them respond.
- **Sell the outcome, not the hours:** *"This automation saves you ~1 day a week.
  At your time's worth, it pays for itself in a month."*
- **Offer a smaller first project** if they're unsure — land them, over-deliver,
  then grow the relationship. The second project is always easier to sell.
- **Walk-away line:** if someone wants a $2,000 build for $300, politely decline.
  Cheap clients are usually the most work. *"I don't think I'm the right fit for
  that budget, but happy to help if scope changes."*

### Phrases to keep ready
- *"My price for that scope is $___, fixed. Want me to put it in writing?"*
- *"I can work to your budget — let's adjust what's included."*
- *"Happy to do a smaller version first so you can see how I work."*

---

## 5. Upsells & recurring revenue (where the real money is)

After every project, offer:
- **Maintenance retainer** — keeps the site/automation healthy, recurring income.
- **Phase 2** — *"Now that the site's live, want the booking automation too?"*
- **Analytics / reporting** add-on.
One happy client paying monthly beats chasing five new ones.

---

## 6. Pricing across countries

Clients in the US / UK / Western Europe / Australia / Gulf pay more than local
clients — that's normal, not unfair. Quote in **their** currency comfort zone
(usually USD). Don't undercut yourself just because you're remote; you're
competing with agencies that charge 5–10×.

---

> **Mindset:** you're not "a kid who codes." You're a service that makes
> businesses money and saves them time. Price like it, deliver like it.
